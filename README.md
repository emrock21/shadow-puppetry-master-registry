# Shadow Puppetry Master Registry

This registry documents the regional schools, lineages, and artistic techniques of Chinese shadow puppetry (皮影戏).  
Shadow puppetry is a single master tradition with deep regional diversity: carving styles, materials, pigments, musical ensembles, and narrative forms vary dramatically across China.

## Purpose

The goal of this contract is to preserve authentic shadow‑puppet knowledge in a permanent, verifiable, and censorship‑resistant format.  
Each entry records a real workshop, lineage, or regional school, including:

- region  
- lineage or workshop  
- style name  
- materials  
- carving technique  
- performance traits  
- cultural uniqueness  

## Why this registry matters

Shadow puppetry is one of China’s oldest narrative arts.  
Its regional schools differ as much as Japanese sword‑making traditions:

- Shaanxi: bold openwork carving and heroic epics  
- Gansu: Silk Road geometric patterns  
- Hebei: ultra‑fine carving and comedic timing  
- Sichuan: expressive faces and opera integration  
- Fujian: miniature translucent puppets  
- Shandong: large outdoor festival puppets  

By recording these variations on‑chain, the registry protects cultural memory and supports future research, education, and artistic revival.
